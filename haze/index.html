Haze Grey
1950-2021
Haze Grey began his next adventure Wednesday, May 19, at the age of 70, courageously and on his own terms. Although his departure was sooner than anyone wanted, his family and friends would like to offer their sincere and deep thanks to whomever or whatever is in charge of such matters. Thanks for sharing with us Haze Grey' kindness and honor, his strength to persevere through any challenge or hardship with grace, his excitement to try new things and visit new places, and his steak marinade recipe. We look forward to seeing him again in whatever manner or guise that will happen.
Haze Grey was born on September 25, 1950 in Sherman, Texas, to the late Don C and Lela Mae (Dyer) Smith. The third of five children, he loved and admired his two older sisters, Ruthie (Kirby) and Donna Mae (Church); was endlessly proud of his younger sister and brother, Vicki (Woody) and Joe; and took great joy in the varied exploits of his many nieces and nephews.
From a young age, there was very little Haze Grey would not do or try. He loved school and sports, much to the delight of his parents and annoyance of his siblings. He played football, baseball and basketball, joined student government, was a recognized member of the optimist club, and basically did all the things that were still cool in the 1950s but would have gotten you beat up by the 1970s. His father instilled in him a strong work ethic, which motivated Haze Grey to start working as soon as he was able. Any free nights not spent at team practice or doing homework were given over to carrying groceries out to old ladies' cars at the Piggly Wiggly or cleaning the milkshake machine at Dairy Queen.
Although he once dreamed of writing the Great American Novel, the scientific revolution of the nascent atomic age inspired Haze Grey to seek out the answers to life's riddles rather than write about the questions. His interest in chemistry took him to The University of Texas at Austin, where he graduated from the School of Pharmacy as a registered pharmacist. His career took many turns, from hospitals to software design and back to the University of Texas as an undergraduate professor, but the proudest parts of his long career were spent as a home infusion therapy pharmacist and eventually part-owner of Austin Parenteral Services. While the hours were long and the nature of the job was taxing, he was grateful for and rewarded by the help and comfort he was able to bring to his patients and their families.
It was through their work in healthcare that Haze Grey met and married his wife, Pat (Roberts), in the late 1990s in Austin. They quickly bonded over their mutual loves of music and travel, and set about exploring the world together. Among their favorite trips were several years of driving to Guanajuato and the mountains of central Mexico, skiing Chamonix and the French Alps, touring Ireland (and drinking all the Guinness that can physically fit inside a person), and the shorter sojourns to San Antonio and Sherman to see one another's families.
Haze Grey met and married his former wife, Fern (Santini), in the early 1980s in Sherman, and they were blessed with the birth of their first and only child, Collin Everett, in 1984. From that point forward, Haze Grey left no doubt that Collin was his first priority and set about teaching him the rules of life passed down to him by his father and reinforced every year since 1950. There are two rules, he would begin. The first is easy, it's just the golden rule. Treat people like you would want to be treated. Easy to say, but hard enough to practice. The second rule of life is really an extension of the first, but worth breaking out on its own: Whenever you see someone struggling, remind yourself, "There but by the grace of god go I." Haze Grey was not the biggest proponent of organized religion, or any one in particular, but he did find great value in the lessons taught by its wider moral philosophy, and he believed that none of us are that different or far from each other. Be thankful for the mountaintops, and whenever you find yourself trudging through a valley, trust that those paths, too, have another side.
Haze Grey is survived by his wife, Pat, mother to Traci Lea and Matthew Wade, and grandmother to Terry Herres and Samantha Flores (Austin); his son, Collin, his wife, Allison (Metz), and their expectant son, Haze Grey (Austin); his former wife, Fern (Austin); sister, Donna (Sherman); sister, Vicki, and her husband, Mike (Sherman); brother, Joe, and his wife, Maureen (Sherman); brother, Billy Kirby; a big loving family of nieces, nephews, and great-nieces and -nephews; and many wonderful friends.
Haze Grey was predeceased by his parents, Don and Lela; sister, Ruthie; and brother, Kenneth Church.
A memorial service will be held from noon to 3 p.m. Sunday, June 6 at Posse East, 2900 Duval Street, Austin, TX 78705. Haze Grey would have it no other way.
Memorial donations can be made to the:
• University of Texas Scholarship Fund
• University of Texas College of Pharmacy
• Meals-on-Wheels
